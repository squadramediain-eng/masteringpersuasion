Motion language — the studio's animation rules (applied to every frame & embedded in the manifest)

Cue-synced to narration

Every content element enters on its VO cue word — the SVG carries data-cue / data-t (absolute sec) / data-relt (sec into the scene) / data-enter. Source of truth: motion/cue-bindings.json. The gantt below plots each entrance at its real time in the scene.

Signature easing

All content entrances use the brand curve cubic-bezier(0.16,1,0.3,1). Ambient loops use sine-in-out; continuous wave/orbit use linear. Typewriter runs linear.

Entrance rhythm

Layers reveal back-to-front by data-layer: bg → env → waves → decor → scene → char → icon → card → text. World layers (bg/env/waves) are present at t=0; everything above enters on its cue.

Text — titles type on

Headlines typewriter per-char with a visible caret (~22 cps), two-tone: head #0840a5 + emphasis #6084f0. Body copy fades/rises (translateY 26→0), never types.

Numerals

Section numeral scales up — scale 0.82→1 + fade, 640ms — a confident hero beat, no bounce.

Concept & spotlight discs

Disc pops from centre scale 0.6→1 with a 1.06 overshoot; where a dashed orbit exists it rotates 0→360° on a 24s linear loop; glow fades in behind.

Characters

Grouped one figure per person (ab, bosun, leader, officer_*, crew_*) with named limbs. Slide in from the nearest edge + fade, 640ms, then a slow idle float ±6px.

Ship & water

Ship (scene) sails in along the waterline (900ms) then bobs ±8px / ±1°. Wave layers drift horizontally ±14px, back layers slower — the water signature, looping the whole frame.

Arrows & connectors

Draw on via stroke-dashoffset in flow direction, ~520ms, staggered along the path; arrowheads pop at the end.

Stat number

Count-up + scale pop 0.7→1, ~950ms, with the sanctioned top-lit gradient shimmer. It owns the frame; icons orbit quietly.

Alert cue

The only non-blue beat: hazard-X / spark / cross-badge pops in late scale 0→1 with a 2-cycle shake, drawing the eye to the risk. Never decorative.

One continuous world

The film never cuts — the waves band & env sea life persist across every frame; only the furniture above changes. Transitions slide/fade over the shared water, through a ~0.5–1.5s breath where the outgoing furniture clears and the next title types on.

Ghost placeholders

Every frame is complete at t=0: low-opacity ghost slots reserve every space content will fill, so the screen is never blank while it waits. Content enters into its ghost on its VO cue word; the ghost fades as it lands.

Lag budget

Transform & opacity only; ≤3 concurrent entrances (stagger the rest); world layers are drawn once and never re-rendered; heavy donor art is flattened.

Frame transitions

No cut, no wipe, no full-frame cross-dissolve. Outgoing furniture slides/fades out as incoming slides in over the unbroken wave band; section numerals wipe in as chapter markers. Connectors draw to a slot before its content lands.
